# Go-to-Market Strategy — AI Spend Audit

---

## Target Persona

**The AI Budget Owner** — A CTO, Engineering Manager, or technical founder at a startup with 3-50 developers who subscribes to 2+ AI tools (Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude) and suspects they're overpaying but doesn't have time to audit every plan and seat. They manage $200-$5,000/month in AI tool spend and feel a vague anxiety that they're leaving money on the table. They are active on X (Twitter), read Hacker News, and trust peer recommendations over vendor marketing.

---

## First 100 Users Strategy

**Channel: X (Twitter) DMs and reply threads**

I will identify and personally DM 100 CTOs and engineering leaders who have publicly posted about AI tool costs, developer productivity, or startup budgets. The DM will be 2 sentences: "Hey [name], I built a free tool that audits your AI tool spend and finds hidden savings. Most teams find $200-500/month. Want to try it? Takes 3 minutes."

Why this works:
- **Warm outreach** — I'm only DMing people who've expressed the pain point publicly
- **Low commitment** — "3 minutes" is the key promise; the tool requires no login
- **Built-in sharing** — Every audit result has a shareable URL with the savings amount in the OG title, making it natural to post

**Goal:** 100 DMs → 30 audits → 15 email captures → 5 high-savings consultations → 1-2 Credex pipeline leads.

---

## Unfair Channel Advantage

I have direct access to the Credex network of startup founders and CTOs. Credex's existing customer base is precisely the persona that needs this tool: technical leaders managing growing AI budgets. I can distribute the tool through:
- The Credx newsletter (2,000+ technical subscribers)
- Direct introductions to Credex portfolio companies
- Credex Slack community posts

This is an unfair advantage because the distribution is *warm* and *pre-qualified*. These are people who already trust the Credex brand and are predisposed to try a tool that Credex recommends.

## Content & SEO Plan

**Week 1-2: Launch content**
- "We audited 50 startups' AI tool spend. Here's what we found" (blog post with aggregate data)
- "Cursor Pro vs GitHub Copilot: The real cost comparison for 2025" (SEO-targeted comparison page)
- "How a 10-person startup saved $4,800/year on AI tools" (case study format)

**Week 3-4: SEO foundation**
- Build comparison pages for every tool pair: "ChatGPT vs Claude pricing," "Copilot vs Cursor cost," "Windsurf vs Cursor for teams"
- Target long-tail keywords: "how much does cursor cost per month," "copilot business vs enterprise," "ai tool budget for startups"
- Each comparison page links to the audit tool as the "find out which is cheaper for YOUR team" CTA

**Ongoing:**
- Monthly "AI Tool Pricing Update" post (links back to audit tool)
- X threads with specific savings examples ("This startup was paying $780/month for AI tools. We got them to $440. Here's how:")
- Hacker News submission when we hit 500 audits completed (social proof milestone)

---

## Conversion Funnel

```
Awareness (X threads, SEO, DMs)
    ↓ ~10% click-through
Audit Tool Landing Page
    ↓ ~40% complete audit
Audit Results (shareable)
    ↓ ~20% email capture
Lead in Database
    ↓ ~15% high-savings flag
Consultation CTA → Credex Pipeline
```

The key metric is **audit completion rate** — if people start the form but don't finish, the entire funnel breaks. The 3-minute promise and "no login required" badge are critical for keeping this rate above 35%.
